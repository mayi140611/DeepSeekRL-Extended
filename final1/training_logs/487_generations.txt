###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Mrs. Smith wanted to buy wears worth $500. She went to a boutique with the $500 but by the time she had picked out everything she liked, she realized that she would need two-fifths more money than she had. If the shop owner gave her a discount of 15%, how much more money will she still need?

#### ANS ####

95
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####

system
Initially, Mrs. Smith needed $500 worth of wears. She needed two-fifths more, which amounts to \( \frac{2}{5} \times 500 = 200 \). Therefore, the total amount she needed was \( 500 + 200 = 700 \) dollars.

The shop owner gave a 15% discount, so she only needed to pay \( 700 - 0.15 \times 700 = 700 - 105 = 595 \) dollars.

She initially had $500, so the additional amount she needs is \( 595 - 500 = 95 \) dollars.

</reasoning>
<answer>
95
</answer>
