###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

James decides to build a tin house by collecting 500 tins in a week. On the first day, he collects 50 tins. On the second day, he manages to collect 3 times that number. On the third day, he collects 50 tins fewer than the number he collected on the second day. If he collects an equal number of tins on the remaining days of the week, what's the number of tins he collected each day for the rest of the week?

#### ANS ####

50
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####

system
<reasoning>
On the first day, James collects 50 tins. 
On the second day, he collects 3 times that number, which is 3 * 50 = 150 tins. 
On the third day, he collects 50 tins fewer than the number he collected on the second day, which is 150 - 50 = 100 tins. 
So, in the first three days, James collects a total of 50 + 150 + 100 = 300 tins. 
To reach the total of 500 tins in a week, he needs to collect 500 - 300 = 200 tins on the remaining days.
Since the remaining days are 4 (because there are 7 days in a week and 3 days have already been accounted for), he will collect 200 / 4 = 50 tins each day for the rest of the week.

Therefore, on the fourth to seventh day, James collects 50 tins each day.
</reasoning>
<answer>
50
</answer>
