###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

There were 18 students assigned in a minibus for a field trip. Eight of these students were boys. On the day of the field trip, the number of girls and boys was the same since some of the girls were not able to join the trip. How many girls were not able to join the field trip?

#### ANS ####

2
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
Let's begin by figuring out the number of girls initially assigned to the minibus. We know that there were 18 students in total and 8 of those were boys. To find the number of girls, we subtract the number of boys from the total number of students:

\[ \text{Number of girls} = \text{Total number of students} - \text{Number of boys} \]
\[ \text{Number of girls} = 18 - 8 \]
\[ \text{Number of girls} = 10 \]

Given that on the day of the field trip, the number of girls and boys was the same, we can deduce that there must have been an equal number of girls and boys joined the trip.

Since we know that there are 8 boys, the number of girls joining the trip must equal 8 to make the number of boys and girls join the trip the same. Therefore, the number of girls that were not able to join the field trip is the initial number of girls minus the number who joined:

\[ \text{Number of girls not joining} = \text{Number of initial girls} - \text{Number of girls joining} \]
\[ \text{Number of girls not joining} = 10 - 8 \]
\[ \text{Number of girls not joining} = 2 \]

</reasoning>
