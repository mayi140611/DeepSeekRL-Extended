###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Cupcakes are sold in packages of 10 and 15. Jean bought 4 packs of 15 cupcakes.  If she will give one cupcake each to 100 children in the orphanage, how many packs of 10 cupcakes should she need to buy?

#### ANS ####

4
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
<reasoning>
To determine how many packs of 10 cupcakes Jean needs to buy, we first calculate the total number of cupcakes she will need to give to the children. Since she will give one cupcake each to 100 children, she requires 100 cupcakes.

First, we calculate how many cupcakes Jean will have from the packs she already has:
\[ 4 \text{ packs} \times 15 \text{ cupcakes per pack} = 60 \text{ cupcakes} \]

Next, we need to find out how many more cupcakes Jean needs to buy:
\[ 100 \text{ cupcakes} - 60 \text{ cupcakes} = 40 \text{ cupcakes} \]

Each pack of 10 cupcakes provides 10 cupcakes, so Jean needs to buy:
\[ \frac{40 \text{ cupcakes}}{10 \text{ cupcakes per pack}} = 4 \text{ packs} \]

Therefore, Jean should buy 4 packs of 10 cupcakes.
</reasoning>
<answer>
4
</answer>
