###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Punger collects baseball cards.  He buys 60 packs of baseball cards. Each pack has 7 cards inside. He wants to put these cards in special pages to protect the cards. Each page can hold 10 cards. How many pages does he need to buy?

#### ANS ####

42
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####

system
To determine how many pages Punger needs to buy, we first need to calculate the total number of baseball cards he will have.

Punger buys 60 packs of baseball cards, and each pack contains 7 cards. Therefore, the total number of cards is:

\[ 60 \text{ packs} \times 7 \text{ cards/pack} = 420 \text{ cards} \]

Next, we need to find out how many pages he needs to hold all 420 cards, given that each page can hold 10 cards. We do this by dividing the total number of cards by the number of cards each page can hold:

\[ \frac{420 \text{ cards}}{10 \text{ cards/page}} = 42 \text{ pages} \]

Since the result is an integer, we do not need to round up; 42 pages will exactly hold all the cards.

Thus, Punger needs to buy \(\boxed{42}\) pages.
