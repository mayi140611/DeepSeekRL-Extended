###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Betty is saving money for a new wallet which costs $100. Betty has only half of the money she needs. Her parents decided to give her $15 for that purpose, and her grandparents twice as much as her parents. How much more money does Betty need to buy the wallet?

#### ANS ####

5
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
Betty initially needs $100 for the wallet.

Since she has only half of it, she currently has:
\[ \frac{100}{2} = 50 \text{ dollars} \]

Her parents give her $15, so now she has:
\[ 50 + 15 = 65 \text{ dollars} \]

Her grandparents give her twice as much as her parents, so:
\[ 2 \times 15 = 30 \text{ dollars} \]

Adding this to what she already has:
\[ 65 + 30 = 95 \text{ dollars} \]

To find out how much more money Betty needs, we subtract what she has from the total cost of the wallet:
\[ 100 - 95 = 5 \text{ dollars} \]

Therefore, Betty still needs $5 more to buy the wallet.

</reasoning>
