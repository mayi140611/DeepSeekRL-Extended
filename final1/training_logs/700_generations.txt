Sabrina gave 10 cookies to her brother. Her mother gave Sabrina half the number of cookies she gave her brother. Then Sabrina gave two-thirds of her cookies to her sister. If Sabrina had 20 cookies at the start, how many cookies are left with her?

\[

\[
\text{Total cookies after receiving from mother} = 20 + 5 = 25 \text{ cookies}

\[

\[
\text{Cookies given to sister} = 16 \text{ cookies}
\]

Finally, we calculate the number of cookies left with Sabrina by subtracting the cookies she gave to her sister from the total cookies she had before giving any away:

\[
\text{Cookies left} = 25 - 16 = 9 \text{ cookies}
