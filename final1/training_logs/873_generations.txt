###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Kimberly went strawberry picking with her family over the weekend. She picked 8 times the amount of strawberries her brother picked and her parents picked 93 strawberries less than her. If her brother picked 3 baskets each containing 15 strawberries, how many strawberries would they each have if they divide the total number of strawberries equally amongst them?

#### ANS ####

168
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
<reasoning>
First, we need to determine how many strawberries Kimberly's brother picked. Since he picked 3 baskets each containing 15 strawberries, he picked a total of \(3 \times 15 = 45\) strawberries.

Kimberly picked 8 times the amount her brother picked, so she picked \(8 \times 45 = 360\) strawberries.

Her parents picked 93 strawberries less than Kimberly, so they picked \(360 - 93 = 267\) strawberries.

Now, we add up the total number of strawberries each person picked:
- Kimberly: 360 strawberries
- Her brother: 45 strawberries
- Her parents: 267 strawberries

The total number of strawberries they picked is \(360 + 45 + 267 = 672\) strawberries.

They need to divide this total equally among the 3 of them, so each person would get \(672 \div 3 = 224\) strawberries.

Therefore, each of them would have 224 strawberries if they divide the total number of strawberries equally amongst them.
</reasoning>
<answer>
224
</answer>
