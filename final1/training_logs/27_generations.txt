###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Brennan was researching his school project and had to download files from the internet to his computer to use for reference. After downloading 800 files, he deleted 70% of them because they were not helpful. He downloaded 400 more files but again realized that 3/5 of them were irrelevant. How many valuable files was he left with after deleting the unrelated files he downloaded in the second round?

#### ANS ####

400
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system

\[70\% \text{ of } 800 = 0.70 \times 800 = 560\]
So, he deleted 560 files. This means he had:
\[800 - 560 = 240\]
valuable files left after the first round.

Next, calculate the number of files Brennan deleted in the second round:
\[3/5 \text{ of } 400 = \frac{3}{5} \times 400 = 240\]
So, he deleted 240 files. This means he had:
\[400 - 240 = 160\]
valuable files left after the second round.

Therefore, the total number of valuable files after both rounds of deletions is:
\[240 + 160 = 400\]
valuable files.

So, the answer is 400.
