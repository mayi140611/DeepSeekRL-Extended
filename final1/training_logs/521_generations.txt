###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

One barnyard owl makes 5 hoot sounds per minute.  If 5 less than 20 hoots per minute are heard coming out of the barn, how many Barnyard owls would this noise be coming from?

#### ANS ####

3
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
To solve this, first, let's determine the total number of hoots per minute when 5 less than 20 hoots are heard. If 20 hoots come from the barn, then 5 less than that is 15 hoots (20 - 5 = 15). 

Next, we know that one barnyard owl makes 5 hoots per minute. If the total hoots heard is 15, and one owl makes 5 sounds, then we can determine the number of owls by dividing the total number of hoots by the number one owl makes:

\[ \text{Number of owls} = \frac{\text{Total hoots heard}}{\text{Hoots per owl}} = \frac{15}{5} = 3 \]

So, this noise from the barn is coming from 3 barnyard owls.

</reasoning>
