###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Gerald spends $100 a month on baseball supplies. His season is 4 months long. He wants to use the months he's not playing baseball to save up by raking, shoveling, and mowing lawns. He charges $10 for each. How many chores does he need to average a month to save up for his supplies?

#### ANS ####

5
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
To determine how many chores Gerald needs to average each month, we first need to calculate the total amount of money he needs to save up for his baseball supplies. Since his season is 4 months long and he spends $100 a month, the total amount of money needed is:

\[ 100 \text{ dollars/month} \times 4 \text{ months} = 400 \text{ dollars} \]

Next, we know that he will be saving up this amount in the 8 months (12 months in a year minus his 4-month baseball season, which includes the months he is not playing). To find out how many chores he needs to average each month, we divide the total amount of money by the number of months:

\[ \frac{400 \text{ dollars}}{8 \text{ months}} = 50 \text{ dollars/month} \]

Since he charges $10 for each chore, we can find the number of chores he needs to do each month by dividing the amount he saves each month by the cost per chore:

\[ \frac{50 \text{ dollars/month}}{10 \text{ dollars/chore}} = 5 \text{ chores/month} \]

Therefore, Gerald needs to average 5 chores a month to save up for his baseball supplies.
