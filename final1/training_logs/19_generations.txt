###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Tim rides his bike back and forth to work for each of his 5 workdays.  His work is 20 miles away.  He also goes for a weekend bike ride of 200 miles.    If he can bike at 25 mph how much time does he spend biking a week?

#### ANS ####

16
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####

system
Given that Tim bikes back and forth to work for 5 workdays, his daily bike ride is 20 miles. Since he does this back and forth, each day he bikes \(20 \text{ miles out} + 20 \text{ miles back} = 40 \text{ miles}\). Over 5 workdays, this totals \(40 \text{ miles/day} \times 5 \text{ days} = 200 \text{ miles}\).

Additionally, Tim has a weekend bike ride of 200 miles. So, the total distance Tim bikes in a week is \(200 \text{ miles (work rides)} + 200 \text{ miles (weekend ride)} = 400 \text{ miles}\).

With a biking speed of 25 mph, we can calculate the time he spends biking per day and for the week:

\[ \text{Time per day} = \frac{\text{Distance per day}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{40 \text{ miles}}{25 \text{ mph}} = 1.6 \text{ hours} \]

\[ \text{Total time per week} = \text{Daily time} \times \text{Number of days in a week} = 1.6 \text{ hours/day} \times 7 \text{ days} = 11.2 \text{ hours} \]

Therefore, the total time Tim spends biking in a week is 11.2 hours.
