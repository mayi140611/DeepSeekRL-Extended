###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

In a conference room, 40 chairs with a capacity of 2 people each were arranged in rows in preparation for the board meeting of a company, whose number of members was the same as the chairs' capacity. If 2/5 of the chairs were not occupied, and the rest each had two people, calculate the number of board members who did attend the meeting.

#### ANS ####

48
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
Let's denote the number of chairs as \( C \) and the number of board members as \( B \). Given that each chair has a capacity of 2 people, so \( B = 2C \).

We are also informed that 2/5 of the chairs were not occupied, and the rest had 2 people each. So, 3/5 of the chairs were occupied.

The number of chairs occupied is \( \frac{3}{5} \times C \), which is given to be equal to the number of board members \( B \).

Now we have \( B = 2C \) and \( B = \frac{3}{5} \times C \).

Setting these equal to each other:

\[
2C = \frac{3}{5} \times C
\]

Dividing both sides by \( C \) (assuming \( C \neq 0 \)):

\[
2 = \frac{3}{5}
\]

This equation is true and no need for further reasoning since the setup gives us the direct relationship.

Now, solving for \( B \):

\[
B = 2C = 2 \times 20 = 40
\]

So, the number of board members who attended the meeting is \( 40 \).

</reasoning>
