###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

A boxer weighs 97 kg at 4 months from a fight. He is on a diet that allows him to lose 3 kg per month until the day of the fight. How much will he weigh on the day of the fight?

#### ANS ####

85
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
To calculate the boxer's weight on the day of the fight, we subtract the total weight loss from his initial weight.

First, let's calculate the total weight lost by the day of the fight:
- The boxer is on a diet that allows him to lose 3 kg per month.
- There are 4 months until the day of the fight.

Total weight loss = 3 kg/month * 4 months = 12 kg

Now, let's subtract the total weight loss from his initial weight:
- Initial weight = 97 kg
- Total weight loss = 12 kg

Weight on the day of the fight = Initial weight - Total weight loss
Weight on the day of the fight = 97 kg - 12 kg = 85 kg

Thus, the boxer will weigh 85 kg on the day of the fight.
