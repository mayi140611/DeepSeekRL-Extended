###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Stella and Twinkle are filling up a truck with a capacity of 6000 stone blocks at the rate of 250 blocks per hour per person. They work for four hours and are then joined by 6 other people who also work at the same rate. How many hours did filling the truck take?

#### ANS ####

6
#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####


system
Let's break down the problem step by step:

1. Stella and Twinkle fill the truck at a rate of 250 blocks per hour per person.
2. They both work for 4 hours, so they contribute:
   \(250 \text{ blocks/hour/person} \times 4 \text{ hours} \times 2 \text{ people} = 2000 \text{ blocks}\).

3. The truck's capacity is 6000 blocks, and after 4 hours, they have filled 2000 blocks. So remaining blocks are:
   \(6000 - 2000 = 4000 \text{ blocks}\).

4. At this point, 6 more people join, making a total of 8 people.

5. The new rate for the 8 people is:
   \(250 \text{ blocks/hour/person} \times 8 \text{ people} = 2000 \text{ blocks/hour}\).

6. The time taken for the remaining 4000 blocks is:
   \(4000 \text{ blocks} / 2000 \text{ blocks/hour} = 2 \text{ hours}\).

7. Therefore, the total time taken to fill the truck is:
   \(4 \text{ hours} (initial) + 2 \text{ hours} (remaining) = 6 \text{ hours}\).

</reasoning>
<answer>
6
</answer>
