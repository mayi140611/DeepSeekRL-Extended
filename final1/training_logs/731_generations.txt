###### ORIGINAL PROMPT #####

Marcos has to get across a 5 mile lake in his speedboat in 10 minutes so he can make it to work on time. How fast does he need to go in miles per hour to make it?

#### GENERATION 1 RESPONSE ####

<reasoning>
To calculate the speed in miles per hour that Marcos needs to maintain, we can use the formula:
\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} \]

Given:
- Distance = 5 miles
- Time = 10 minutes

Since speed is typically expressed in miles per hour, we need to convert 10 minutes into hours by dividing by 60. So, 10 minutes is \( \frac{10}{60} = \frac{1}{6} \) hours.

Now we can plug in the values into the speed formula:
\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{5 \text{ miles}}{\frac{1}{6} \text{ hours}} \]

We can simplify this equation:
\[ \text{Speed} = 5 \times 6 = 30 \text{ miles per hour} \]

So, Marcos needs to maintain a speed of 30 miles per hour to cross the 5 mile lake in 10 minutes.
</reasoning>
